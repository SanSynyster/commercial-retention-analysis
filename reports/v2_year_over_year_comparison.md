# V2 Year-Over-Year Comparison

## Purpose

This report compares the two trading periods inside the expanded Online Retail II dataset.

The goal is to understand whether retention, revenue concentration, and repeat behaviour are stable across years or whether the current one-year baseline differs materially from the prior-year period.

Periods compared:

| Period | Date Range |
| --- | --- |
| Prior year | 2009-12-01 to 2010-11-30 |
| Current year | 2010-12-01 to 2011-12-09 |

The current-year period aligns with the original one-year project baseline.

---

## Commercial Interpretation

The year-over-year comparison strengthens the project's core story.

Key findings:

- Repeat behaviour is consistently important across both periods.
- Second purchase conversion is stable, moving only from 66.5% to 65.6%.
- Median days to second purchase is also stable, moving from 49 to 50 days.
- Repeat-order revenue share remains high in both periods, but is slightly lower in the current year.
- Customer concentration increases in the current year, with top-decile revenue share rising from 59.6% to 61.5%.
- Identifiable revenue coverage falls from 86.3% to 83.1%, meaning the current year has a larger anonymous revenue share.

This suggests the current baseline is not an outlier. The retention and concentration patterns are visible in both years.

---

## Strategic Meaning

The year-over-year comparison supports the same CRM operating model as the baseline project:

1. Retention matters because repeat behaviour drives most identifiable revenue in both years.
2. Lifecycle acceleration remains relevant because median second purchase timing is consistently around 50 days.
3. High-value customer protection remains important because revenue concentration is persistent and slightly higher in the current year.
4. Data-quality context remains important because identifiable revenue coverage declined in the current year.

---

## Final Takeaway

The expanded dataset does not materially change the commercial interpretation. It reinforces it.

One-line conclusion:

> Across both years, repeat revenue is strong, second-purchase timing remains slow enough to justify lifecycle intervention, and revenue concentration remains high enough to justify high-value lapse prevention.
