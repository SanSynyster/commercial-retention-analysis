# KPI Definitions

## Scope Note (Important)
All lifecycle and retention KPIs are calculated on **identifiable customers only** (non-null CustomerID), representing ~84% of analytical purchase revenue. Anonymous transactions are excluded from customer-level metrics.

---

## Gross Purchase Revenue
Revenue from valid positive purchase transactions only.

Definition:
- Quantity > 0
- UnitPrice > 0
- Not cancelled invoice

---

## Net Transactional Revenue
Gross purchase revenue plus refund/cancellation transactions.

Interpretation:
- Used for reconciliation with Finance
- Includes negative quantities and cancelled invoices

---

## Analytical Purchase Revenue
Revenue used for lifecycle and retention analysis.

Definition:
- Same as Gross Purchase Revenue
- Excludes refunds and cancellations

Note:
This is intentionally **not net of refunds**, as lifecycle analysis focuses on behavioural purchasing activity.

---

## Identifiable Customer Revenue
Revenue linked to transactions with non-null CustomerID values.

Definition:
- Analytical Purchase Revenue where CustomerID is not null

---

## Valid Order
A transaction grouped at invoice level that represents a legitimate purchase.

Definition:
- At least one valid positive purchase line
- Not a cancelled invoice
- Positive quantity and price

---

## Valid Customer
Customer with:
- Non-null CustomerID
- At least one valid order

---

## One-Time Customer
Valid customer with exactly one valid order.

---

## Repeat Customer
Valid customer with more than one valid order.

---

## First-Order Revenue
Revenue generated from the first valid order of each customer.

---

## Repeat-Order Revenue
Revenue generated from all orders after the first order (order_rank > 1).

---

## One-Time Customer Revenue
Total revenue generated by customers who only placed one order.

---

## Repeat Customer Revenue
Total revenue generated by customers who placed more than one order.

Note:
Includes both first and subsequent purchases from repeat customers.

---

## Retained Customer
Valid customer with at least one valid positive purchase in a later month after acquisition month.

---

## Second Purchase Conversion
Percentage of valid customers who place a second order.

Definition:
- Customers with order_rank >= 2 / total valid customers

---

## Days to Second Purchase
Number of days between first and second order.

Definition:
- Second order date minus first order date

---

## Customer Concentration (Top 10%)
Percentage of total identifiable revenue generated by the top 10% of customers ranked by revenue.

---


## Refund / Cancellation Revenue
Revenue from transactions where:
- Quantity < 0
- OR invoice is marked as cancelled

Note:
Tracked separately and excluded from analytical purchase revenue.

---

# Sprint 2 — CRM Lifecycle & Experimentation Definitions

## Customer Acquisition Definition
A customer is considered acquired once they complete their first valid purchase.

Note:
Acquisition status is independent from refund-adjusted profitability or long-term retention behaviour.

---

## Lifecycle Acceleration
Lifecycle acceleration refers to reducing the time between first and second purchase.

Commercial objective:
- Increase retention momentum
- Accelerate revenue realisation
- Encourage earlier repeat behaviour

---

## Customer Lifecycle Velocity
Measure of how quickly customers progress through repeat purchasing stages.

Example indicators:
- Days to second purchase
- Time between repeat orders
- Repeat cadence

---

## CRM Trigger Window
Recommended lifecycle timing range for CRM intervention.

Current V1 working ranges:
- Day 7–14: early engagement
- Day 14–30: second purchase acceleration window
- Day 30–60: replenishment / reminder window
- Day 60–90: at-risk period
- 90+ days: winback eligibility

Note:
Trigger windows are behavioural guidance ranges, not fixed operational rules.

---

## At-Risk Customer
Customer who:
- Has only one valid order
- Has not repeated within the expected repeat window

Current V1 interpretation:
- 30–90 days since first purchase without repeat activity

---

## Active Repeat Customer
Customer with:
- More than one valid order
- Recent purchase activity

Current V1 interpretation:
- Repeat customer with purchase activity within ~60 days

---

## Lapsed Customer
Previously active customer with no recent purchase activity.

Current V1 interpretation:
- No valid purchase for 90+ days

---

## High-Value Customer
Customer belonging to the highest revenue concentration segment.

Current V1 interpretation:
- Top revenue decile (~top 10%)

Note:
High-value classification functions as a priority tag rather than a mutually exclusive lifecycle segment.

---

## Second Purchase Accelerator Campaign
Lifecycle campaign designed to accelerate transition from first purchase to second purchase.

Primary objective:
- Increase second purchase conversion
- Reduce days to second purchase

Current MVP trigger:
- Day 21 after first purchase if no repeat order exists

---

## Incrementality
Commercial value generated because of the intervention, above the level of naturally occurring customer behaviour.

Measured using:
- Exposed vs control comparison
- Longer-term behavioural separation
- Incremental revenue estimation

---

## Cannibalisation
Situation where campaign-driven purchases would likely have occurred naturally later without intervention.

Example:
- Customer purchases at Day 25 instead of natural Day 50 behaviour.

---

## Gross Uplift
Observed increase in behavioural or revenue metrics in the exposed group.

Examples:
- Higher conversion rate
- Earlier repeat purchase timing
- Increased short-term revenue

---

## Net Incremental Value
Commercial value remaining after accounting for:
- natural customer behaviour
- control-group performance
- discount cost
- operational cost
- cannibalisation risk

---

## Holdout / Control Group
Customer group intentionally excluded from the CRM intervention.

Purpose:
- Estimate natural customer behaviour
- Measure incremental impact
- Reduce attribution bias

---

## Guardrail Metrics
Metrics monitored to ensure campaigns do not create negative side effects.

Examples:
- Unsubscribe rate
- Spam/complaint rate
- Discount dependency
- Negative revenue efficiency

---

## Revenue Efficiency
Commercial value generated relative to intervention cost.

Typical considerations:
- Incremental revenue
- Discount spend
- Operational cost
- Estimated contribution margin

---

## Customer Productivity
Commercial value generated per acquired customer.

Example indicators:
- Repeat-order revenue
- Revenue per customer
- Purchase frequency
- Long-term retention value

---

## Concentration Risk
Business dependency on a relatively small portion of customers for revenue generation.

Current V1 indicator:
- Revenue contribution from top customer deciles

Interpretation:
Concentration is not automatically negative, but elevated dependency increases vulnerability to churn within high-value customer groups.
